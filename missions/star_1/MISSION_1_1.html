Mission 1.1: Instant Setup

Right now, millions of people are playing games that started exactly where you are about to start: with a blank screen and an idea. Roblox has over 70 million daily players, and every single game they play was built by someone using the same free tool you are about to download. Today, you join the ranks of Roblox developers.

In the next 15 minutes, you will go from having nothing to pressing Play on your very first Roblox project. No experience required. No tricks. Just follow along, and by the end of this mission, you will have a working game environment running on your screen.

Fun Fact: Some of the most popular Roblox games earn their creators millions of dollars per year. Every one of those developers started exactly like you - opening Roblox Studio for the first time and wondering what to click.

Your Mission

Learning Objective: Download and install Roblox Studio, open a baseplate project, and locate the Play/Stop buttons to test your game.

What You Will Accomplish

  • Create a Roblox account (if you do not have one)
  • Download and install Roblox Studio
  • Open the Baseplate template
  • Find and use the Play button to test your game
  • Find and use the Stop button to return to editing

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Create Your Roblox Account

If you already have a Roblox account, skip to Step 2. Otherwise, let us get you set up.

  1. Open your web browser and go to roblox.com
  2. Click the Sign Up button
  3. Fill in your birthday, username, and password
  4. Complete the verification (you may need to verify your email)
  5. Write down your username and password somewhere safe!

Tip: Choose a username you will be proud of - other players and your classmates will see it! Avoid using your real name for privacy.

Step 2: Download Roblox Studio

Roblox Studio is completely free. It is the official tool that all Roblox games are made with.

  1. Go to roblox.com/create
  2. Click Start Creating
  3. If prompted, log in with your Roblox account
  4. Click Download Studio
  5. Run the installer file that downloads
  6. Wait for the installation to complete (this may take a few minutes)

Note for School Computers: If you get a message saying you cannot install software, raise your hand and let your teacher know. They may need to help you or contact IT.

Step 3: Open Roblox Studio

Once installation finishes, Roblox Studio should open automatically. If it does not:

  • Windows: Search for "Roblox Studio" in the Start menu
  • Mac: Find "Roblox Studio" in your Applications folder

When Studio opens, you will see a home screen with templates and recent projects. This is your launching pad for creating games!

Step 4: Create a New Baseplate Project

A Baseplate is the simplest possible Roblox world - just a flat ground and a place for players to spawn. It is perfect for learning.

  1. On the home screen, look for the New tab or section
  2. Find the template called Baseplate (it looks like a gray flat surface)
  3. Click on Baseplate to create a new project
  4. Wait a moment while your project loads

When it loads, you will see a 3D view of a large gray floor stretching into the distance. Congratulations - you are looking at your first game world!

Explore: Try clicking and dragging in the 3D view. Hold the right mouse button and move your mouse to look around. Use the scroll wheel to zoom in and out. Get comfortable with moving your view - you will do this constantly!

Step 5: Find the Play and Stop Buttons

The most important buttons in Roblox Studio are Play and Stop. They let you instantly test your game.

  1. Look at the top of the screen for the Home tab (it should already be selected)
  2. In the toolbar, find the Play button - it is a blue button with a play triangle
  3. The Stop button is right next to it - it is a red square

Visual Guide: The Play button is in the "Test" section of the Home tab toolbar. It looks like this: a blue circle with a white triangle pointing right (like a video play button).

Step 6: Play Your First Game!

Time for the magic moment. Let us run your game!

  1. Click the Play button
  2. Watch as the view changes - you are now a player in your game!
  3. Use W, A, S, D to walk around
  4. Press Space to jump
  5. Move your mouse to look around
  6. When you are done exploring, click the Stop button to return to editing

Try It!

Walk to the edge of the baseplate and jump off. What happens? (Do not worry, nothing breaks - this is how you discover how Roblox works!)

Important: Always click Stop before trying to build or change anything. If you try to edit while the game is running, your changes might not save properly!

Quick Reference: Essential Controls

Action Control
Move (in Play mode) W, A, S, D
Jump (in Play mode) Space
Look around (in Play mode) Move mouse
Rotate camera (in Edit mode) Right-click + drag
Zoom (in Edit mode) Scroll wheel
Play the game F5 (or click Play)
Stop the game Shift + F5 (or click Stop)

Mission 1.4: Victory Part

Think about the last time you finished a level in a video game. There was probably a door, a flag, a glowing portal, or some kind of finish line that screamed "YOU MADE IT!" That moment of triumph does not happen by accident - game designers create it deliberately. Now you are going to create that moment for your players.

Your obstacle course has platforms to jump on and danger to avoid. But right now, the last platform looks exactly like all the others. How would a player know they won? In this mission, you will learn how visual design communicates meaning in games, and create a victory zone that players will feel proud to reach.

Design Psychology: Colors have meaning. Red often means danger or stop. Green means go or success. Blue feels calm. Gold feels rewarding. Every choice you make communicates something to your players, even if they do not consciously notice it.

Your Mission

Learning Objective: Add a distinctively colored finish line part that marks the completion point of your obstacle course.

What You Will Create

  • A victory platform with a distinctive, rewarding color
  • Visual contrast that makes the goal obvious
  • A complete game loop: start, challenge, danger, goal

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Identify Your Finish Line Location

Before we add anything, let us think about where the victory part should go.

  • Click Play and complete your obstacle course
  • When you land on the last platform, look around - where should the finish line be?
  • Click Stop and select your last platform

We have two options:

  1. Option A: Turn your last platform INTO the victory zone (change its color)
  2. Option B: Add a NEW part on top of or next to your last platform

For this mission, we will do Option A - transforming your final platform into a victory zone. This keeps things simple and clear.

Step 2: Change the Platform Color

Let us make that final platform stand out!

  1. Select your last platform (the one players reach at the end)
  2. Open the Properties panel (View > Properties if you do not see it)
  3. Find the BrickColor property
  4. Click on it to open the color picker
  5. Choose a color that says "victory!" Some great options:
    • Bright green - Classic "win" color
    • Gold / Bright yellow - Feels rewarding
    • Lime green - Energetic success
    • Cyan - Cool and distinct

Contrast is Key: Your victory color should be completely different from your other platforms. If your regular platforms are gray, almost any bright color works. The goal is that players can see the finish line from the start!

Step 3: Give It a Clear Name

Just like we named the kill brick, let us give this part a descriptive name.

  1. With the victory platform still selected
  2. Look at the Properties panel
  3. At the very top, find the Name property
  4. Change it from "Part" to VictoryPlatform or FinishLine

Why Names Matter: In later missions, you will write scripts that detect when players reach the finish line. Having clear names like "VictoryPlatform" makes your code much easier to write and understand!

Step 4: Add Extra Visual Polish (Optional)

Want to make your victory zone even more special? Here are some properties you can experiment with:

Material

In Properties, find Material and try these options:

  • Neon - Glows brightly (great for victory zones!)
  • SmoothPlastic - Clean, polished look
  • Foil - Shiny metallic surface
  • Glass - Transparent and futuristic

Transparency

Values from 0 (solid) to 1 (invisible). Try 0.3 for a semi-transparent effect.

Reflectance

Values from 0 (matte) to 1 (mirror). Try 0.5 for a shiny surface.

Try It!

Set your victory platform to Material = Neon and see how it glows! Neon material emits light and looks amazing as a finish line. If it is too bright, change the color to a darker shade.

Step 5: Check the Visual Flow

Good game design means players understand what to do just by looking. Let us test that.

  1. Click Play
  2. Look at your course from the starting position
  3. Ask yourself: Can I clearly see where I need to go?
  4. The visual path should be: Gray platforms > Red danger below > Green/Gold victory at the end

Your course should now tell a visual story:

  • Gray = Normal, safe to stand on
  • Red = Danger, do not touch!
  • Green/Gold = Goal, get here to win!

Congratulations! You just applied real game design principles. Professional game designers spend a lot of time thinking about visual communication - what colors mean, what draws the eye, what feels rewarding. You are already doing that work!

Step 6: Consider Size and Position

A few final thoughts on your victory platform:

  • Size: The victory platform can be a bit larger than other platforms - it is the reward, after all! Players should feel like they are landing somewhere special.
  • Height: Consider making it slightly higher than the previous platform, so reaching it feels like an achievement.
  • Visibility: Make sure it is visible from the start. If your course curves, the player should still be able to see that green glow in the distance.

Quick Reference: Part Appearance Properties

Property What It Does Suggested Values
BrickColor The color of the part Bright green, Gold, Lime green
Material The surface texture/look Neon (glows), SmoothPlastic, Foil
Transparency How see-through it is 0 (solid) to 1 (invisible)
Reflectance How shiny/mirrored it is 0 (matte) to 1 (mirror)
CastShadow Whether it creates shadows true/false

  • The victory platform is visible from the starting position
  • My course has clear visual contrast: normal platforms, danger zone, victory zone

Every published game, from small student projects to massive AAA titles, has a launch day. Today is yours. You've designed, built, tested, polished, and prepared. You've turned an empty Roblox Studio workspace into a complete experience. Now it's time to share it.

There's something special about clicking that publish button for the first time. It's a mix of excitement, nervousness, and pride. You're putting your creative work out into the world where real people will play it. That takes courage, and it's exactly what game developers do every time they launch something new.

You didn't just follow a tutorial. You created something that exists, that others can experience, that's permanently part of the Roblox platform. That's real development.

Developer Moment: Many professional developers remember their first published game more vividly than later successes. That first launch—no matter how small the game—is a milestone that marks the transition from learning to creating. Today, you become a published game developer.

Mission Objective

Publish your island runner game to the Roblox platform, upload your thumbnail and assets, set appropriate permissions, and verify that your game is live, accessible, and working correctly for players. You'll obtain and test your shareable link to ensure others can find and play your creation.

Part 1: The Publishing Process

Step 1: Final Studio Check

Before you publish, open your game one last time in Roblox Studio and do a quick verification.

  • Save your work: Press Ctrl+S to ensure everything is saved
  • Quick test: Press F5 and do a 30-second playthrough to confirm nothing broke
  • Check Output: Look for any error messages in the Output window
  • Verify assets: Make sure all your sounds, effects, and scripts are working

If anything seems off, fix it now. Once published, you can update, but it's better to launch with everything working correctly.

Step 2: Publishing to Roblox

Now you're ready to push your game to the Roblox platform. The publishing process uploads your entire game to Roblox's servers where it can be accessed by players.

Publishing Your Game

  1. Click "File" → "Publish to Roblox"
    • If this is your first publish, it will say "Publish to Roblox"
    • If you've published before, it will say "Publish to Roblox As..."
  2. Choose publishing option:
    • Select "Create new game..." if this is your first time
    • Or select your existing game to update it
  3. Verify your settings:
    • Game name appears correctly
    • Description is complete
    • Genre is set appropriately
  4. Set initial visibility:
    • For classroom launches, you might start with "Private" or "Friends Only"
    • This lets you test before opening to everyone
    • You can change to "Public" later
  5. Click "Create" or "Save"
    • The upload process will begin—this may take 30 seconds to a few minutes
    • Don't close Studio during the upload
    • You'll see a progress indicator
  6. Wait for confirmation:
    • You'll get a message when publishing is complete
    • Studio will show "Saved to Roblox" in the title bar
First Publish vs. Updates: Your first publish creates the game on Roblox's servers and gives it a unique Game ID. Future publishes are updates to that same game. Players who favorite your game will automatically get updates when you publish new versions.

Step 3: Uploading Your Thumbnail

Your thumbnail won't upload automatically with the game—you need to add it through the Roblox website. This is a crucial step because your game won't look appealing without it.

Adding Your Thumbnail

  1. Open your web browser and go to www.roblox.com
  2. Log in to your Roblox account (if not already logged in)
  3. Click "Create" in the top navigation bar
  4. Find your game in the list of creations
    • It should be at the top if you just published it
    • Look for the name you gave it
  5. Click on your game to open its management page
  6. Look for the "Icon" or "Thumbnails" section
  7. Click "Upload Image" or the add thumbnail button
  8. Select your thumbnail file from where you saved it
  9. Confirm the upload
    • Roblox may take a few moments to process the image
    • Make sure it appears correctly and isn't cut off oddly
  10. Set as primary if you upload multiple thumbnails

Thumbnail Upload Requirements

  • File size: Maximum 10MB (most screenshots are under 2MB)
  • Format: PNG or JPG/JPEG
  • Recommended resolution: 1920x1080 or 1280x720
  • Aspect ratio: 16:9 works best
  • Content: Must follow Roblox community standards (no inappropriate content)

Part 2: Configuring Your Live Game

Accessing Game Configuration

While you're on your game's page on the Roblox website, you'll configure additional settings that control how players discover and interact with your game.

Key Settings to Configure

Basic Settings Tab:

  • Game Name: Confirm it's correct
  • Description: Paste your polished description from Mission 7.2
  • Genre: Set to Adventure, Platformer, or most appropriate category
  • Playable Devices: Usually keep all devices enabled (Computer, Phone, Tablet, Console)

Access Settings Tab:

  • Who Can Play:
    • Private: Only you can play (for initial testing)
    • Friends: You and your friends (good for classroom first launch)
    • Public: Anyone can play (full launch)
  • Age Restrictions: Usually "All Ages" for a runner game

Monetization Tab (Optional):

  • You can skip this for your first launch
  • If interested, you could add Passes or Developer Products later
  • Focus on getting players first before monetizing

When to Go Public

You have two strategies for launching:

Strategy 1: Soft Launch (Recommended for Classroom)

  1. Publish as "Private" or "Friends Only" first
  2. Test the live version yourself
  3. Share with a few classmates for feedback
  4. Fix any issues discovered in the live environment
  5. Then switch to "Public" for full launch

Advantage: Safer, lets you catch any issues before public exposure

Strategy 2: Full Launch

  1. Publish directly as "Public"
  2. Share immediately with everyone
  3. Monitor and update if issues arise

Advantage: Immediate full exposure, maximizes launch momentum

Part 3: Verification and Testing

Getting Your Game Link

Every published Roblox game has a unique URL that players use to access it. You'll need this link to share your game and verify it works.

Finding Your Game Link

  1. On your game's page on the Roblox website, look at the URL in your browser's address bar
  2. It will look something like: https://www.roblox.com/games/[NUMBER]/[Your-Game-Name]
  3. Copy this entire URL—this is your game's permanent link
  4. The number is your Game ID (unique identifier for your game)

Alternative method:

  1. From your game's configuration page, look for a "View on Site" or similar button
  2. This takes you to the public-facing game page
  3. Copy the URL from there

Testing Your Published Game

Now it's time to verify everything works in the live environment. Sometimes things behave differently when published versus in Studio.

Published Game Test Checklist

Test 1: Accessibility

  1. Open a new browser tab or window
  2. Paste your game link and press Enter
  3. Verify the game page loads correctly
  4. Check that your thumbnail appears
  5. Read your description—does it display correctly?
  6. Check that the Play button is visible and clickable

Test 2: Launching the Game

  1. Click the green "Play" button on your game page
  2. Wait for Roblox to launch (it should open in the Roblox Player app)
  3. If prompted, allow the browser to open Roblox
  4. Wait for the game to load
  5. Verify you spawn in the correct location

Test 3: Gameplay Verification

  • ☐ Spawn location is correct and safe
  • ☐ All obstacles and platforms are present
  • ☐ Controls work (WASD movement, Space to jump)
  • ☐ All scripts are functioning (moving obstacles, collectibles, etc.)
  • ☐ Sound effects play correctly
  • ☐ Lighting and visual effects appear as expected
  • ☐ Win condition works when you reach the end
  • ☐ Respawn works if you fall or die

Test 4: Performance Check

  • Does the game run smoothly? (Check FPS in the Roblox menu)
  • Are there any lag spikes or freezing?
  • Do parts load correctly without disappearing?
  • Does the game feel responsive to input?

Testing from a Different Account (Optional but Recommended)

If possible, have a friend or classmate try accessing your game from their account. This verifies that permissions are set correctly and others can actually play.

External Access Test

  1. Share your game link with a classmate or friend
  2. Ask them to click the link and try to play
  3. If they can't access it, check your Access Settings:
    • Make sure "Who Can Play" is set to "Friends" (if they're your friend) or "Public"
    • Verify no age restrictions are blocking them
  4. Watch them play (if in the same room) or ask for feedback
  5. Confirm they can complete the game without issues

Part 4: Post-Launch Monitoring

Checking Game Statistics

Once your game is live, Roblox provides statistics about how it's performing. While you won't have many players immediately, it's good to understand what metrics are available.

Where to Find Stats

  1. Go to www.roblox.com/develop
  2. Click on your game
  3. Look for tabs like "Stats", "Analytics", or "Performance"

Key Metrics to Understand

  • Visits: Total number of times players have joined your game
  • Playing: How many players are currently in your game
  • Favorites: How many players have favorited your game
  • Likes: Ratio of likes to dislikes (shows player satisfaction)
  • Average Play Time: How long players stay in your game
What Good Metrics Look Like: For a student project runner, don't expect thousands of visits. Instead, focus on quality metrics: Are the people who play it liking it? Do they stay for the full experience? Are classmates and friends enjoying it? Those matter more than raw numbers.

Responding to Issues

If you discover problems in the live version or players report bugs, you can always update your game.

How to Update Your Published Game

  1. Open your game in Roblox Studio
  2. Make your fixes or changes
  3. Test in Studio to verify the fix works
  4. Click "File" → "Publish to Roblox"
  5. Select your existing game (don't create a new one)
  6. Click "Save" to publish the update
  7. The live version updates immediately—players already in the game will get the update when they rejoin
Version Control: Consider keeping notes of what changes you make with each update. This helps track improvements and debug issues if something breaks. Many developers maintain a changelog documenting each update.

Part 5: Sharing Your Game

Ways to Share

Now that your game is live and verified working, it's time to get players. Here are effective ways to share your creation.

Direct Link Sharing

  • Copy your game URL
  • Share via text, email, or messaging apps
  • Post in Discord servers or social media (if age-appropriate)
  • Add to your Roblox profile description

Roblox Social Features

  • Invite Friends: Use the in-game invite system to bring friends into your server
  • Join Friends: When you're playing your game, friends can see and join you
  • Groups: If you're in Roblox groups, share your game there
  • Profile: Your game appears on your Roblox profile for others to discover

Classroom Sharing

  • Share your link in your class chat or assignment submission
  • Prepare for the showcase presentation (next mission)
  • Create a QR code linking to your game for easy classroom access

Creating a QR Code (Optional)

A QR code makes it easy for classmates to access your game on their phones.

  1. Go to a free QR code generator like qr-code-generator.com
  2. Select "URL" as the type
  3. Paste your game link
  4. Generate and download the QR code
  5. Include it in your presentation or print it for classroom sharing
